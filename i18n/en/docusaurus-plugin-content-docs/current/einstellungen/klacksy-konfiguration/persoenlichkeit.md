# Klacksy Personality

Klacksy doesn't have a rigid, hard-coded character — under Settings > Klacksy Personality you define in your own words how Klacksy behaves, speaks, and reacts to situations.

## How it works

**Six text fields:** The personality is organized into six sections, each with its own free-text field:

- **Identity** — Who is Klacksy? Role, purpose, and context.
- **Personality & drive** — Character, basic attitude, what motivates Klacksy.
- **Tone of voice** — How does Klacksy sound? Formal or casual, brief or detailed.
- **Boundaries** — What is Klacksy not allowed to do? Privacy, taboo topics, sensitive areas.
- **Communication style** — Structure of responses, how it handles uncertainty, follow-up questions.
- **Values & continuity** — Guiding values and how Klacksy evolves over time.

**Saving:** Each field is saved automatically on blur (when it loses focus) — a dedicated save button isn't needed. Changes take effect immediately in the next chat with Klacksy.

## Good to know

- The personality belongs to the shared default Klacksy agent — a change affects **all** users, not just your own account.
- The fields are pure free text — there are no formatting requirements; short, clear sentences tend to work more reliably than long embellishments.
- Empty fields aren't a problem: Klacksy then falls back on its built-in default attitude. A few further internal behavior aspects are maintained by the system itself and aren't directly configurable here.
